References

References used specifically in the development of the OGD Handbook are listed here.

A growing number of documents have been released as part of the Open Government Data (OGD) Switzerland project, corresponding to the work areas of the implementation of the national OGD Strategy. These are primary sources for the OGD Handbook, which you can view in condensed form and download here:

Visit the Library

The Open Government Data Strategy for Switzerland and supporting studies provide additional background.

International references

The Open Data Handbook is a primary inspiration for this project, of note are the history and structure of the community effort. It was initially developed by the Open Government Data Working Group of Open Knowledge, and is now maintained by a network of contributors:

  • Visit OpenDataHandbook.org (Repository, Wiki, Mailing list)
  • See Localised Guides at the Share-PSI working group of W3C for links to more guides and handbooks.

The KDZ Process is a source of reference for workflow development and continuous evaluation of OGD policy in EU E-Government projects.

  • KDZ Open Government Vorgehensmodell
  • KDZ Stadt Wien presentation (Slideshare)

A forerunner of OGD in Switzerland, the Swiss association for Open Data Opendata.ch has a manifesto based on mutually agreed principles relevant to this project, regularly maintained feeds with currents and analysis, and community resources:

  • Opendata.ch Manifest
  • Opendata.ch News (also in French)
  • Swiss Open Data community wiki

Commonly cited

  • The Open Definition is often cited in open data research, and its principles are fundamental to OGD.
  • The Global Open Data Index is a crowdsourced effort to evaluate open data availability around the world.
  • The Open Data Barometer run by the World Wide Web Foundation is an annual expert analysis of the relative openness of data and its critical impact on government, economy and society.
  • Tim Berner-Lee's 5 stars of linked open data (unofficial site) has seen widespread adoption in explaining technical openness.
  • The World Bank's Open Government Data Toolkit has primers and resources for Open Data initiatives.
